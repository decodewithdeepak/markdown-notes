## **1. Marketing Management**

> PYQ: What is the difference between Marketing and Selling? Explain all concepts of marketing. (Jul-21, 15 marks)  
> PYQ: Characterise the marketing management and also define its functions. What is the importance of marketing management and market research? (May-23, 15 marks)

### 1.1 Definition of Marketing

**Marketing** is the process of identifying customer needs, creating products to satisfy those needs, and delivering them to customers at the right price, place, and time while making profit.

**Philip Kotler:** "Marketing is a social and managerial process by which individuals and groups obtain what they need and want through creating, offering and exchanging products of value with others."

### 1.2 Marketing vs Selling

> PYQ: What is the difference between Marketing and Selling? Explain all concepts of marketing. (Jul-21, 15 marks)

| Aspect | Marketing | Selling |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| **Focus** | Customer needs | Product |
| **Objective** | Customer satisfaction | Sales volume |
| **Starting Point** | Customer | Factory/Product |
| **Approach** | Pull (attract customers) | Push (force products) |
| **Time Horizon** | Long-term relationship | Short-term transaction |
| **Scope** | Wider (research to distribution) | Narrower (only selling) |
| **Example** | Research needs, then create product | Make product, then convince to buy |

### 1.3 Marketing Concepts

> PYQ: Explain all concepts of marketing. (Jul-21, 15 marks)

**1. Production Concept (Before 1930s):**

Focus on producing goods at low cost and making them widely available.

**Philosophy:** "Customers prefer affordable and easily available products"

**Example:** Henry Ford's Model T - mass production for low cost

**2. Product Concept (1930s-1950s):**

Focus on making quality products with superior features.

**Philosophy:** "Customers prefer products with best quality and features"

**Limitation:** Marketing Myopia - focusing too much on product, ignoring customer needs

**3. Selling Concept (1950s-1960s):**

Focus on aggressive selling and promotion.

**Philosophy:** "Customers won't buy unless we push them"

**Example:** Insurance companies, unsought goods

**4. Marketing Concept (1960s-1980s):**

Focus on understanding and satisfying customer needs.

**Philosophy:** "Customer is king. Find needs and fulfill them"

**Example:** Companies conducting market research before launching products

**5. Societal Marketing Concept (1980s onwards):**

Focus on customer needs while considering society's well-being.

**Philosophy:** "Satisfy customers while protecting society and environment"

**Example:** Eco-friendly products, reducing plastic packaging, CSR

### 1.4 Functions of Marketing

> PYQ: Functions of marketing manager (May-23, 3 marks)

**1. Market Research:** Gathering information about customers, competitors, market

**2. Product Planning:** Deciding what products to offer, developing them

**3. Pricing:** Determining the right price

**4. Branding and Packaging:** Creating brand identity and attractive packaging

**5. Promotion:** Advertising, sales promotion, personal selling, PR

**6. Distribution:** Making products available through channels

**7. Selling:** Personal interaction to convince customers

**8. Customer Service:** After-sales support, handling complaints

**9. Financing:** Providing credit facilities (EMI, installments)

**10. Market Information:** Collecting and providing market intelligence

### 1.5 Importance of Marketing Management

**1. Customer Satisfaction:** Understands and meets customer needs

**2. Increases Sales:** Effective promotion reaches more customers

**3. Competitive Advantage:** Differentiates from competitors

**4. Business Growth:** Expands market, new products, profitability

**5. Brand Building:** Creates strong brand recognition and trust

**6. Adapts to Changes:** Monitors trends, responds to market changes

## **2. Marketing Research**

> PYQ: Explain the term market research and its process. (May-23, 15 marks)  
> PYQ: Explain the importance of marketing research in present industrial scenario. (Jul-22, 7.5 marks)

### 2.1 Meaning

**Marketing Research** is the systematic gathering, recording, and analyzing of information about problems related to marketing of goods and services.

### 2.2 Objectives

**1. Identify Problems and Opportunities:** Find market problems, discover opportunities

**2. Understand Customers:** Customer needs, preferences, buying behavior

**3. Analyze Competition:** Competitor strategies, market share, strengths/weaknesses

**4. Forecast Demand:** Predict future sales, market potential

**5. Test Marketing Strategies:** Evaluate effectiveness before full launch

**6. Reduce Risk:** Make informed decisions, avoid costly mistakes

### 2.3 Importance of Marketing Research

> PYQ: Explain the importance of marketing research in present industrial scenario. (Jul-22, 7.5 marks)

**1. Better Decision Making:** Provides factual information, reduces guesswork

**2. Understands Customer Needs:** Know what customers want, identify preferences

**3. Identifies Market Opportunities:** Find new markets, untapped segments

**4. Reduces Risk:** Test before full launch, avoid failures

**5. Monitors Competition:** Track competitor moves, stay ahead

**6. Forecasts Trends:** Predict future demand, market changes

**7. Improves Marketing Effectiveness:** Evaluate campaigns, optimize spending

**8. Product Development:** Design better products, add desired features

### 2.4 Limitations

**1. Costly:** Requires significant investment, small businesses may not afford

**2. Time-Consuming:** Takes weeks or months, may delay decisions

**3. Limited Scope:** Cannot predict future with certainty, human behavior unpredictable

**4. Subjective:** Respondents may not be honest, biased responses

**5. Rapidly Changing Markets:** Research becomes outdated quickly

### 2.5 Process of Marketing Research

> PYQ: Explain the term market research and its process. (May-23, 15 marks)

**Step 1: Define the Problem**

Clearly identify what needs to be researched.

**Example:** "Why are sales declining?"

**Step 2: Develop Research Plan**

Decide how to conduct research.

- Research design (Exploratory, Descriptive, Causal)
- Data sources (Primary or Secondary)
- Research methods (Survey, Observation, Experiment)
- Sample size and selection
- Budget and timeline

**Step 3: Collect Data**

**Primary Data (Collected fresh):**
- Surveys (questionnaires)
- Interviews (personal, telephone, online)
- Observation (watching behavior)
- Experiments (test marketing)
- Focus groups

**Secondary Data (Already available):**
- Company records
- Government publications
- Industry reports
- Internet sources

**Step 4: Analyze Data**

Process and interpret collected information.

- Organize data
- Use statistical tools
- Find patterns and trends
- Draw insights

**Step 5: Present Findings**

Communicate results to management.

- Prepare report with charts, graphs, tables
- Clear recommendations
- Presentation to decision makers

**Step 6: Make Decision**

Use research findings to take action.

- Implement recommendations
- Monitor results

**Flow Diagram:**

```
Define Problem → Develop Plan → Collect Data → 
Analyze Data → Present Findings → Make Decision
```

## **3. Management Information System (MIS)**

> PYQ: Explain the characteristics in an ideal MIS. (Jul-22, 7.5 marks)

### 3.1 Meaning

**MIS** is a system that collects, processes, stores, and distributes information to support management decision-making.

### 3.2 Characteristics of Ideal MIS

**1. Accuracy:** Information should be correct, no errors, reliable

**2. Timeliness:** Available when needed, real-time or near real-time

**3. Relevance:** Useful information related to decision at hand

**4. Completeness:** All necessary information provided, no missing data

**5. Simplicity:** Easy to understand, user-friendly interface

**6. Flexibility:** Can adapt to changing needs, customizable

**7. Cost-Effective:** Benefits outweigh costs, good ROI

**8. Security:** Data protected from unauthorized access, backup systems

**9. Integration:** Links different departments, shares information

**10. User-Friendly:** Easy to operate, minimal training needed

## **4. Advertising**

> PYQ: Define the term 'Advertising'. Explain the sources of advertising which a company can adopt to increase their sale. (Jul-21, 15 marks)  
> PYQ: Define advertising. Explain the functions and criticism of advertising with the help of examples. (Jul-22, 15 marks)  
> PYQ: Criticize Advertising. (Jul-22, 2.5 marks)

### 4.1 Meaning of Advertising

**Advertising** is a paid form of non-personal communication about products, services, or ideas by an identified sponsor through mass media.

**Key Elements:**
- **Paid:** Advertiser pays for space/time
- **Non-personal:** Reaches mass audience, not face-to-face
- **Identified Sponsor:** Advertiser is known
- **Mass Media:** TV, radio, newspapers, internet

### 4.2 Objectives of Advertising

**1. Create Awareness:** Inform people about product, make them aware of brand

**2. Increase Sales:** Persuade people to buy, boost revenue

**3. Build Brand Image:** Create positive perception, brand recognition

**4. Differentiate from Competitors:** Show unique features, stand out

**5. Remind Customers:** Keep brand in mind, maintain presence

**6. Educate Consumers:** Provide information, how to use product

### 4.3 Functions of Advertising

> PYQ: Explain the functions and criticism of advertising. (Jul-22, 15 marks)  
> PYQ: Role of advertising in marketing (Jul-21, 3 marks)

**1. Informing:** Provides information about products, features, price, availability

**2. Persuading:** Convinces people to buy, highlights benefits, creates desire

**3. Reminding:** Keeps brand in customer's mind, maintains awareness

**4. Creating Demand:** Stimulates desire for products

**5. Building Brand Image:** Creates positive perception, emotional connection

**6. Supporting Salespeople:** Makes selling easier, customers already aware

**7. Differentiating:** Shows how product is different, unique features

### 4.4 Sources/Media of Advertising

> PYQ: Explain the sources of advertising which a company can adopt to increase their sale. (Jul-21, 15 marks)

**1. Television:**
- **Advantages:** Wide reach, audio-visual impact, demonstrates product
- **Disadvantages:** Very expensive, short duration

**2. Newspapers:**
- **Advantages:** Wide circulation, detailed information, flexible, local targeting
- **Disadvantages:** Short life (one day), cluttered

**3. Radio:**
- **Advantages:** Wide reach, low cost, local targeting
- **Disadvantages:** Only audio (no visual), short message

**4. Internet/Digital:**
- **Advantages:** Global reach, cost-effective, targeted, measurable
- **Disadvantages:** Ad blockers, information overload

**5. Social Media:**
- **Advantages:** Viral potential, engagement, cost-effective, real-time feedback
- **Disadvantages:** Negative comments visible, requires monitoring

**6. Outdoor (Billboards, Hoardings):**
- **Advantages:** High visibility, repeated exposure, 24/7 presence
- **Disadvantages:** Limited message, can't target specific audience

**7. Mobile:**
- **Advantages:** Personal device, location-based, high engagement
- **Disadvantages:** Small screen, can be annoying

**8. Magazines:**
- **Advantages:** Quality printing, longer life, targeted audience
- **Disadvantages:** Expensive, limited reach

### 4.5 Criticism of Advertising

> PYQ: Explain the criticism of advertising with examples. (Jul-22, 15 marks)  
> PYQ: Criticize Advertising. (Jul-22, 2.5 marks)

**1. Increases Product Cost:**
- Advertising is expensive, cost added to product price
- Example: Branded products costlier than unbranded

**2. Creates Artificial Needs:**
- Makes people want things they don't really need
- Example: Ads making people feel they need latest phone when current one works

**3. Misleading Claims:**
- Some ads exaggerate or make false claims
- Example: "Lose 10 kg in 10 days" (unrealistic)

**4. Promotes Materialism:**
- Encourages focus on material possessions
- Example: Ads suggesting happiness comes from buying products

**5. Wastes Resources:**
- Money spent on advertising could be used for product improvement
- Example: Millions spent on celebrity endorsements

**6. Encourages Harmful Products:**
- Advertises products bad for health
- Example: Alcohol, tobacco, junk food ads

**7. Stereotyping:**
- Portrays stereotypes about gender, race
- Example: Women shown only in kitchen, fairness cream ads

**8. Information Overload:**
- Too many ads everywhere, annoying, ignored
- Example: 20 ads in 10-minute TV break

**9. Invades Privacy:**
- Tracks user behavior for targeted ads
- Example: Ads following you after searching for product

**10. Manipulates Children:**
- Children can't distinguish ads from reality
- Example: Toy ads making children demand products

**Defense:**
- Provides information for informed choices
- Supports media (newspapers, TV funded by ads)
- Creates jobs
- Promotes competition
- Drives economic growth
