## **1. Introduction to Financial Management**

> PYQ: What is financial management and their objective? (Jul-21, 15 marks)  
> PYQ: What is Financial Management? What is the role of financial manager of a company? (Jul-22, 15 marks)  
> PYQ: What is Financial Management? If you are a finance manager, which type of main decisions you will take? (May-23, 15 marks)

### 1.1 Meaning

**Financial Management** is the planning, organizing, directing, and controlling of financial activities such as procurement and utilization of funds in an organization.

**Simple Definition:** Financial management means managing money in a business - how to get money (sources) and how to use it (investment) to maximize profit.

### 1.2 Scope

**1. Investment Decisions:** Where to invest money (Capital Budgeting)

**2. Financing Decisions:** How to raise money (Debt or Equity)

**3. Dividend Decisions:** How much profit to distribute vs retain

**4. Working Capital Management:** Managing day-to-day finances

---

## **2. Objectives of Financial Management**

> PYQ: What is financial management and their objective? (Jul-21, 15 marks)

### 2.1 Primary Objectives

**1. Profit Maximization:**

Earning maximum profit from business operations.

**Limitations:**
- Ignores time value of money (₹100 today ≠ ₹100 after 5 years)
- Ignores risk
- Short-term focus
- May compromise quality

**2. Wealth Maximization (Modern Objective):**

Maximizing the market value of shares or wealth of shareholders.

**Advantages:**
- Considers time value of money
- Considers risk
- Long-term focus
- Sustainable growth

**Why Better:** Long-term perspective, risk-return balance, shareholder value creation

### 2.2 Other Objectives

**1. Ensuring Adequate Funds:** Sufficient money when needed

**2. Proper Utilization:** Use funds efficiently, no wastage

**3. Ensuring Safety:** Minimize risk, protect capital

**4. Sound Capital Structure:** Right mix of debt and equity

## **3. Functions of Financial Management**

> PYQ: What is financial management and their objective? (Jul-21, 15 marks)

**1. Financial Planning:** Estimating needs, preparing budgets

**2. Financial Control:** Monitoring, comparing actual vs planned, corrective action

**3. Investment Decisions (Capital Budgeting):**

> PYQ: Capital Budgeting (Jul-21, 3 marks; May-23, 3 marks)

**Capital Budgeting** is evaluating and selecting long-term investments.

- Deciding where to invest
- Fixed assets or current assets
- Techniques: Payback Period, NPV, IRR
- Example: Should we buy new machine for ₹50 lakh?

**4. Financing Decisions:** How to raise funds - debt or equity, capital structure

**5. Dividend Decisions:** How much to distribute vs retain

**6. Working Capital Management:**

> PYQ: Working Capital Management (May-23, 3 marks)

Managing day-to-day finances.

**Working Capital = Current Assets - Current Liabilities**

- Ensure sufficient liquidity
- Meet daily expenses
- Example: Maintain ₹10 lakh cash for operations

**7. Cash Management:** Ensuring adequate cash availability

**8. Credit Management:** Managing receivables, minimize bad debts

**9. Risk Management:** Identifying and managing financial risks

**10. Financial Reporting:** Preparing financial statements

## **5. Role of Financial Manager**

> PYQ: What is the role of financial manager of a company? (Jul-22, 15 marks)

**1. Financial Planning:** Estimate needs, prepare budgets

**2. Raising Funds:** Identify sources, negotiate with banks/investors

**3. Investment Decisions:** Evaluate proposals, select profitable projects

**4. Cash Management:** Maintain adequate cash, manage flows

**5. Credit Management:** Set credit policies, manage receivables

**6. Dividend Policy:** Recommend dividend rate, balance retention/distribution

**7. Financial Control:** Monitor expenses, take corrective action

**8. Risk Management:** Identify risks, develop mitigation strategies

**9. Financial Reporting:** Prepare statements, ensure compliance

**10. Coordination:** Work with all departments, provide guidance

### 5.1 Financial Management Decisions

> PYQ: If you are a finance manager, which type of main decisions you will take? (May-23, 15 marks)

**As a Financial Manager, key decisions:**

**1. Investment Decisions:**
- Where to invest funds
- Large amounts, long-term impact
- Example: Buy new machinery or not?
- Use NPV, IRR methods

**2. Financing Decisions:**
- How to raise funds - debt or equity
- Affects cost of capital and risk
- Example: Bank loan or issue shares?
- Consider cost, tax benefits, control

**3. Dividend Decisions:**
- Distribute or retain profits
- Affects shareholder satisfaction and growth funds
- Example: Pay 50% dividend or 30%?
- Consider future needs, shareholder expectations

**4. Working Capital Decisions:**
- Managing day-to-day finances
- Ensures liquidity and smooth operations
- Example: How much inventory, cash to maintain?
- Balance liquidity and profitability

**5. Risk Management:**
- Identify and manage financial risks
- Protects from losses, ensures stability
- Example: Hedge foreign exchange risk?
- Cost-benefit analysis

## **6. Capital Structure**

> PYQ: Explain the capital structure and its sources of finance. (Jul-21, 15 marks)  
> PYQ: What do you mean by capital structure? Qualities of optimum capital structure. (Jul-22, 15 marks)  
> PYQ: Capital Structures (May-23, 15 marks)

### 6.1 Meaning

> PYQ: What is the meaning of capital structure? (Jul-22, 2.5 marks)

**Capital Structure** is the mix of debt and equity used by a company to finance its operations.

**Components:**
- **Equity Capital:** Equity shares, preference shares, retained earnings
- **Debt Capital:** Debentures, bonds, long-term loans

**Example:** Company has ₹100 crore capital:
- Equity: ₹60 crore (60%)
- Debt: ₹40 crore (40%)
- **Capital Structure = 60:40**

### 6.2 Importance

**1. Maximizes Returns:** Optimal mix increases profitability, reduces cost

**2. Minimizes Risk:** Balanced structure reduces financial risk

**3. Flexibility:** Ability to raise more funds when needed

**4. Control:** Debt doesn't dilute ownership, equity does

**5. Tax Benefits:** Interest on debt is tax-deductible

### 6.3 Factors Affecting Capital Structure

**1. Cost of Capital:** Choose cheaper source

**2. Risk:** High debt = high risk

**3. Control:** Debt doesn't affect control, equity dilutes

**4. Cash Flow:** Debt requires regular interest payment

**5. Tax Benefits:** Interest is tax-deductible

**6. Market Conditions:** Favorable market = issue equity

**7. Nature of Business:** Stable business = more debt

### 6.4 Optimum Capital Structure

> PYQ: Qualities of optimum capital structure. (Jul-22, 15 marks)

**Optimum Capital Structure** maximizes firm value while minimizing cost of capital.

**Qualities:**

**1. Maximum Returns:** Maximizes earnings per share, highest returns to shareholders

**2. Minimum Cost:** Lowest weighted average cost of capital (WACC)

**3. Minimum Risk:** Balanced financial risk, not too much debt

**4. Flexibility:** Can raise more funds when needed

**5. Control:** Maintains ownership control

**6. Tax Benefits:** Utilizes tax shield of debt

**7. Solvency:** Total assets > Total liabilities, financially sound

**Example:** For stable manufacturing company: Equity 50-60%, Debt 40-50%

## **7. Sources of Finance**

> PYQ: Explain sources of finance with pros and cons. (Jul-21, 15 marks)  
> PYQ: Explain various sources of finance in details. (Jul-22, 15 marks)  
> PYQ: Source of Finance (May-23, 3 marks; 15 marks)

### 7.1 Internal Sources

**1. Retained Earnings:**

Profits not distributed as dividend, kept in business.

**Advantages:**
- No cost (no interest or dividend)
- No dilution of control
- Readily available
- Increases creditworthiness

**Disadvantages:**
- Limited amount
- Not available for new companies
- Shareholders may be dissatisfied

**2. Depreciation Funds:**

Money set aside for replacing assets.

**3. Sale of Assets:**

Selling unused or old assets for quick money.

### 7.2 External Sources - Long Term

**1. Equity Shares:**

Ownership capital, shareholders are owners.

**Advantages:**
- No fixed obligation to pay
- Permanent capital
- No charge on assets
- Increases creditworthiness

**Disadvantages:**
- Dilutes control (more owners)
- Expensive (dividend not tax-deductible)
- Complex procedures

**2. Preference Shares:**

Hybrid security - features of both equity and debt.

**Advantages:**
- No dilution of control
- Fixed dividend rate
- No charge on assets

**Disadvantages:**
- Dividend not tax-deductible
- Fixed burden

**3. Debentures/Bonds:**

Long-term borrowing, fixed interest rate.

**Advantages:**
- Interest is tax-deductible
- No dilution of control
- Cheaper than equity
- Trading on equity possible

**Disadvantages:**
- Fixed obligation (must pay interest)
- Charge on assets
- Increases financial risk
- Must be repaid

**4. Term Loans:**

Long-term loans from banks/financial institutions.

**Advantages:**
- No dilution of control
- Interest tax-deductible
- Large amounts available

**Disadvantages:**
- Fixed obligation
- Security required
- Restrictive covenants

**5. Venture Capital:**

Funding for startups and high-growth companies.

**Advantages:**
- Large funds available
- Expertise and guidance
- No repayment burden

**Disadvantages:**
- Dilution of control
- Share profits
- Exit pressure

### 7.3 External Sources - Short Term

**1. Bank Overdraft:**

Withdraw more than bank balance up to a limit.

**Advantages:** Flexible, interest only on amount used

**Disadvantages:** High interest, repayable on demand

**2. Trade Credit:**

Credit from suppliers, buy now pay later.

**Advantages:** No cost if paid on time, easy to obtain

**Disadvantages:** Short period, may lose cash discount

**3. Commercial Paper:**

Short-term unsecured promissory note (15 days to 1 year).

**Advantages:** Cheaper than bank loan, no security needed

**Disadvantages:** Only for large companies, short-term

**4. Factoring:**

Selling receivables to factor for immediate cash.

**Advantages:** Immediate cash, no bad debt risk

**Disadvantages:** Costly, customer relations affected

### 7.4 Comparison

| Source | Cost | Control | Risk | Tax Benefit |
|--------|------|---------|------|-------------|
| Equity | High | Dilutes | Low | No |
| Debentures | Low | No dilution | High | Yes |
| Retained Earnings | Lowest | No dilution | Nil | No |
| Term Loans | Medium | No dilution | Medium | Yes |
